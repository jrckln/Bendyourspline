Details

The aim of this project to is show how nonlinear associations can be estimated with a statistical model using only simple extensions of the basic linear model. Moreover the shiny app allows for comparisons between fractional polynomials, b-splines and natural splines. Parameters and coefficients of each method can be changed and the resulting nonlinear effect is shown. In this way, one can explore how these coefficients effect the functional form of the association between the explanatory variable and an outcome of interest.

Data

Data was obtained from various online sources using an R script. The R script can be downloaded with the “Download” button on the right in tab Methods under CODE.

Average TISS - Mean blood pressure

The SUPPORT study dataset is obtained from http://hbiostat.org/data courtesy of the Vanderbilt University Department of Biostatistics.
It is available in various formats and contains 1000 observations of 35 variables.
The provided dataset is a random sample from Phases I & II of SUPPORT (Study to Understand Prognoses Preferences Outcomes and Risks of Treatment). For more information see here .
The response variable 'avtiss' describes the Therapeutic Intervention Scoring System (TISS), which is a method for calculating costs in the intensive care unit (ICU) and intermediate care unit (IMCU). Mean blood pressure is given in mmHg.

Bmi - Age and Diastolic blood pressure - Age

The data stems from the National Health and Nutrition Health Survey (NHANES). Variables in this data set are participants' BMI (in kg/m²), diastolic blood pressure (in mmHg) and age (in years). Further downloadable variables are participants' weight (in kg), waist circumference (in cm), triglyceride ( in mmol/L), high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol ( in mmol/L), systolic blood pressure (in mm Hg) and others.

Height - Age

Rosner’s FEV data is a dataset of 654 observations of 6 variables. It is obtained from http://hbiostat.org/data courtesy of the Vanderbilt University Department of Biostatistics.
